Key Insights

  • February is consistently the weakest month; Nov-Dec the strongest — classic holiday retail pattern.
  • Promotional months show an 8-18% sales lift over baseline.
  • Sales projected to grow from $95.2K (Jan '26) to $106.4K (Jun '26).
  • 2.62% MAPE is strong accuracy for retail forecasting (under 10% is considered good).

Business Recommendations

  1. Plan inventory and staffing around confirmed seasonality — especially the Nov-Dec peak and February dip.
  2. Formalize a quarterly promotional calendar rather than ad hoc campaigns, since promotions show a measurable, repeatable lift.
  3. Use the confidence interval — not just the point forecast — for budget planning: lower bound for commitments, upper bound for stretch targets.
  4. Feed planned marketing campaigns (from the Attribution project) into future forecasts to improve accuracy further.
  5. Re-run the forecast monthly on a rolling basis to catch trend shifts faster than a static one-time forecast.
